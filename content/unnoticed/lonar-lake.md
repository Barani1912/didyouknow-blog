Lonar Lake, situated in the Buldhana district of Maharashtra, India, is one of the world's best-kept geological secrets. It is the only known hyper-velocity impact crater formed in basaltic rock on Earth. Over 52,000 years ago, a meteor weighing millions of tons struck the region at a speed of 90,000 km/h, leaving behind a massive circular crater nearly 2 kilometers wide.

Today, the crater is filled with a unique, emerald-green lake. What makes Lonar Lake particularly fascinating is that its water is both highly saline and alkaline, hosting a specialized ecosystem of microorganisms found nowhere else on the planet.

In recent years, the lake gained global attention when it mysteriously turned pink overnight due to a sudden bloom of Haloarchaea microbes under high salinity conditions. It is a stunning natural laboratory that continues to surprise researchers studying planetary geology.
