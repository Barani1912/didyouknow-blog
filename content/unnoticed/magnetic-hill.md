Located at an altitude of 14,000 feet above sea level, the Magnetic Hill near Leh in Ladakh, India, is a fascinating phenomenon that attracts curious travelers from all over the world. On this specific stretch of the Srinagar-Leh highway, if you park your vehicle in neutral with the engine turned off, it slowly starts moving uphill on its own, reaching speeds of up to 20 km/h.

For decades, locals and travelers have shared stories of magnetic forces radiating from the mountainside, strong enough to even force passing aircraft to increase their altitude to avoid interference.

However, scientists offer a more rational explanation: the gravity-defying effect is actually an optical illusion. The unique layout of the surrounding mountains and the sloping landscape tricks the human eye, making a slight downhill slope appear to be an uphill climb. Despite the scientific explanation, experiencing your car glide upward on its own remains an unforgettable, magical experience.
