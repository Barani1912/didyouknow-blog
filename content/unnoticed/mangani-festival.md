In the coastal town of Karaikal, Puducherry, a centuries-old tradition transforms the streets into a vibrant sea of yellow and orange. The Mangani Festival (meaning Mango Festival) is an annual celebration dedicated to Karaikal Ammaiyar, one of the three prominent female saints of the 63 Nayanmars in Shaivism.

The highlight of this unique four-day festival is the grand procession of Lord Bikshadana (an avatar of Shiva). As the deity's chariot winds through the crowded streets, devotees standing on rooftops, balconies, and terraces throw ripe mangoes into the crowd. Thousands of people gather below, eagerly trying to catch the flying fruits, which are believed to bring blessings and prosperity.

Over the course of the festival, it is estimated that upwards of 30 tons of mangoes are showered upon the streets. It is a spectacle of joy, community, and devotion that remains largely unnoticed by international tourism, preserving its authentic, local essence.
